Stocks

The Stock Market can be an intimidating force, but it’s not impossible to understand. Here are some its basics.

What is a stock? |

Stocks are basically a representation of a piece of ownership in a company. When you own a stock, you’re considered a shareholder because you ‘share’ a piece of that company along with other people who own their stock. You can purchase as many shares as you want from a company and even purchase parts of a share.


| How do people earn money from the stock market?

The stock market is constantly fluctuating. How much a company’s share is worth can change drastically from $10 to $50 in a day, or it can only change with a 10¢ difference. The value of the company’s shares are fundamentally determined by the profits that the company makes from sales to investors to inflation etc. People earn money the stock market when they buy a company’s shares at a low value then sell their shares when they’re at a higher value.

If you were to buy 1 share of a company for the price of $100, and then during market hours, the value of the share goes up to $120, you can sell that share for $120, thus making a $20 profit. If you were to have 2 shares of that company, you can sell both of them for a $40 profit, so on and so forth.

This act of buying a share and selling it, is what’s considered as a trade.

| When should I buy and when should I sell?

The obvious advice is to buy shares when they’re low and sell shares when they’re high. Although, you can never know for sure when is the ideal time in which a share is considered low or high. This is what makes the stock market intimidating because if you buy a share for $100 and over a year the value of the share goes down to $10, you’ll essentially have lost $90 when you sell those shares. The timeframe in which you buy shares and hold your shares before you sell it varies as well. One of the more risky positions are day traders who trade their stocks within the same day, but it’s relatively considered a gamble in that they trade these high volume shares hoping and needing these shares to go up to sell for profit before the market closes.

For college students, there’s rarely any time to constantly be watching the market, waiting for stock prices to go up to catch the perfect moment to sell. This is why long-term investing is considered more suitable for those who can’t be as dedicated to the market in watching its trends day to day. The mindset of long-term investing involves researching on companies you believe will become more successful in the future, buying some of their shares, and holding onto those shares for years before selling. In most cases if you choose great companies, you’ll likely sell with a substantial amount of profit.

| Why choose long-term investing over short-term?

This debate can be very convincing to the average person when you look at market summaries. If you were to search up a market summary of a company’s stock, you’re usually presented with a huge value number, some percentages, timeline tabs, and a graph. These graphs allow you to visually understand the trend in which a company’s value goes towards, and because the market is so volatile, the trend of a company’s value in a day could go down, but over 5 years, it’s constantly moving upward.

Ex. In the course of 5 days during 2021, Google’s shares went from $2048 to $2002 which is unfortunately a loss. In the course of 5 years, their share was valued at $744 in 2016 then $2097 in 2021. That is a $1353 profit per share you own!

So let’s say you metaphorically started college in 2016 and graduated 2021. If you bought 10 shares of Google at the start of your college career for the price of $7440 in total, you would graduate with that $7440 back in your pocket on top of a $13,530 profit.

In short, long-term investing requires less effort in constant attention to the market as long as you do your occasional, meaningful research, and it can benefit you with greater gains.

Index Funds

It can seem overwhelming to have to choose and research which individual companies and stocks to purchase when dipping your toes into the stock market pool. It requires careful due diligence in which the common student doesn’t have time for or the expertise to go through. This is where index funds come in.

Index funds are basically a group of stocks you invest your money into and you own a small percentage of each of the stocks in the index. It tracks the market as a whole with these multiple companies’ stocks rather than having to only deal with one specific stock. They’re simple to put together, manage, and it’s a way to invest more passively.
